Answer & capture the call

Missed-call text-back that wins the job back

Use it when: a call goes unanswered and you want an instant text that sounds like you — not a robot — so the caller waits for you instead of dialing the next name.

Prompt 
You are an expert copywriter for home-service businesses. Write me 3 versions of a missed-call text-back message to send automatically when I can't answer the phone because I'm out on a job.

My business: [company name]
My trade: [e.g. plumbing, HVAC, landscaping]
My name: [your name]

Rules for every version:
- Under 320 characters so it sends as a single text
- Sounds like a real person, not a call center
- Honestly says I'm out on a job right now
- Asks exactly ONE question: what they need and the address
- Promises a clear next step ("I'll text you back with a time")
- No emojis unless I ask

Give me one casual version, one professional version, and one warm/friendly version, and label each.

Quote fast, follow up

Quote follow-up that gets a yes (or a no)

Use it when: a quote's gone quiet and you want a friendly nudge that moves it off "maybe" — without sounding desperate or chasing.

Prompt 
You are an expert sales copywriter for home-service businesses. Write me a short, friendly follow-up to send when a customer hasn't responded to a quote I sent a few days ago.

The job I quoted: [e.g. kitchen faucet replacement]
Quote amount: [$ amount]
Days since I sent it: [number]
My name / company: [your name, company]

Rules:
- Under 320 characters
- Friendly and zero-pressure — a busy pro checking in, not a desperate salesperson
- Reminds them what the quote was for
- Makes it easy to reply "yes," "no," or "not yet"
- Ends with one clear, low-friction question

Give me 2 versions: one to send by text, one to send by email.
