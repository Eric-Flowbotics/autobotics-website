AI, in plain language

How do I make clean, on-brand graphics for my business without a designer?

A Home Service Edge guide · by Eric Lenhardt

Guides Prompts

Make a deep board. A deep board takes something you'd normally explain in a long paragraph — what's included, how the job works, your process, a before-and-after — and turns it into one clean, single-page graphic in your own colors and logo. You make it by dropping a ready-made prompt into the AI you already use (Claude or ChatGPT) and telling it, in plain words, what you want to explain. It builds the graphic for you. No design software, no code. The first one takes about ten minutes; once it's set up, seconds.

Watch the full walkthrough — or keep reading; it's all written out below.

What a deep board is

A deep board is one clean page that explains a single thing — an explainer, a process, a checklist, how your service works, what's included, or a before-and-after.

It's the opposite of a wall of text nobody reads. Anything you find yourself explaining again and again is a candidate. In the walkthrough I describe it as taking "something you'd normally explain in a long paragraph or maybe say out loud over and over" and turning it into "one clean graphic that somebody actually understands in a couple of seconds."

The name is the finish. The depth, the shadowing, the polish — that's the "deep" in deep boards, and it's what makes one look designed instead of dashed off.

Credit where it's due: the original idea for image-style explainers came from a creator called Ben AI. I thought it was brilliant, sat down with Claude, and together we turned it into something I could reproduce in my own brand, over and over. Ben puts out genuinely good content — worth a look.

Why it's worth doing (you explain the same things all day)

You're the whole business — the sales, the ops, and the actual work — and you explain the same handful of things all day. Turning each one into a graphic you can reuse buys that time back and makes you look professional doing it.

Right now you've got two bad options. Write it all out and hope somebody reads the wall of text. Or make a quick graphic that comes out looking generic — clearly not your business.

There's a deeper reason most operators never fix this: AI still feels like it's not for them. ServiceTitan's 2026 Residential State of the Trades found that 74% of contractors see AI as key to running an efficient business, but only about a quarter are actually using it — and the smallest businesses are the most likely to feel it wasn't built for them. The barrier is understanding, not price. A deep board is one of the lowest-stakes ways across that line: you describe something in plain words, and you get something professional back.

How it works (no code, just the AI you already use)

You drop a ready-made prompt into Claude or ChatGPT, tell it in plain words what you want to explain, and it builds the board for you — you never touch code.

Under the hood, the graphic is code-rendered. That's why it comes out this clean and the text lands exactly right, every word. But you won't write or even see any code. As I put it in the video: "You don't have to write any code. You won't touch code at all... The hard work is done for you."

You've probably tried tools like Excalidraw. They're genuinely good at laying information out, but they take a lot of back-and-forth and won't give you this finished, on-brand look. This is a different animal — and it's not an AI image generator either. Image generators make generic, one-off pictures and tend to garble any text. A deep board comes out polished the first time, with your words intact.

Want to change a word, or anything else on it? Change it and run it again. Seconds.

The ways to use one system

It's one system for anything you need to explain, and every board lands in your brand. A few of the ways it earns its keep:

  • A graphic for a post or an ad — made while you're writing the post, not in a separate design job.
  • A one-page sheet to train a new hire — your process, written down once.
  • A customer handout — how the job works, or what's included, so you're not re-explaining it on every call.
  • A graphic for your website — the same clean look as everything else you put out.

"That's exactly how every graphic in my video gets made," I say in the walkthrough — "while I'm working, not in some separate design job." Once it's set up, it just becomes part of how you work.

Make it look like you (not generic AI)

You set your colors, your font, and your logo one time in a single file. From then on, every board comes out looking like your business — which is why they don't all look the same. It actually looks like you every time, so not generic AI.

Optional, and only if you want to go further: this works even better if you have your own vault — a place like Obsidian that remembers you and how you work across every AI you use. Mine knows my brand, my voice, and my whole business, so the boards come out right without me repeating myself. You do not need one to make great boards. If a vault is new to you, Ben AI (the same creator) has really good content on setting one up.

The one thing to do Monday

Pick the one thing you explain to every customer, write it out like this, and turn it into your first board:

The one thing I explain over and over: ________________
  (e.g., what's included in a standard clean / how install day works)

The 3–6 points I always cover:
  1. ________________
  2. ________________
  3. ________________

Put it in my brand: my colors ________, my logo.

Then grab the free Deep Boards kit at theautobotics.com/get/deep-boards, paste this in behind the kit's prompt, and drop it into Claude or ChatGPT. First board in about ten minutes — no design, no code.

What's next (the upgrade)

Once it's set up, it's part of how you work: writing a post, you make a board; training someone, you hand them the sheet. It takes seconds going forward. The optional vault deepens the consistency over time. But you don't need any of that to make your first one this week — the setup is the only ten-minute part.

FAQ

Do I need to know how to code?

No. A deep board is rendered from code under the hood, which is why it looks clean and the text is exact — but you never touch it. You drop a ready-made prompt into Claude or ChatGPT and describe what you want in plain words.

Do I need a special tool, or does my Claude / ChatGPT work?

The AI you already use is enough. You don't need a new subscription or a separate design app — you paste the kit's prompt into Claude or ChatGPT and go.

How is this different from Canva or an AI image generator?

Image generators make generic, one-off pictures and often garble text. A deep board is a precise, text-accurate, single-page graphic in your own colors and logo — and if you want to change a word, you change it and re-run it in seconds.

Do I need an Obsidian vault?

No — it's optional. A vault makes your results more consistent because the AI remembers your brand and voice, but you can make great boards without one.
